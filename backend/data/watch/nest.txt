Nest thermostats are smart thermostats from Google Nest that help automate and optimize home heating and cooling. They learn your habits, allow remote control through the Google Home app, and can reduce energy usage by adjusting temperatures intelligently.

The lineup mainly includes:

Basic Nest Thermostat
Nest Learning Thermostat
Older Thermostat E models

The most advanced model right now is the 4th Gen Nest Learning Thermostat.

Main Features
Smart Learning

The Learning Thermostat studies your schedule and automatically creates heating/cooling routines over time.

Remote Control

You can control temperature from your phone using the Google Home app, even when away from home.

Energy Saving

Nest provides:

Eco mode
Usage reports
Suggestions for reducing energy consumption

Google claims noticeable HVAC energy savings for many households.

Presence Detection

It detects whether people are home and adjusts temperatures automatically.

Smart Home Integration

Works with:

Google Home
Google Assistant
Matter smart-home ecosystem
Some Alexa integrations

Newer versions support Matter/Thread connectivity.

Multi-room Sensors

Additional Nest Temperature Sensors can help balance temperatures across rooms.

Things to Know Before Buying
Compatibility Matters

Nest thermostats mainly support North American HVAC systems. Some heating systems in Europe and Asia may not be compatible.

C-Wire Requirement

Many newer installations work better with a C-wire for stable power.

Older Models Losing Support

Google ended smart-service support for 1st and 2nd generation Nest Learning Thermostats in late 2025. They still work manually, but app features were removed.

Google Home App Shift

Newer Nest devices primarily use the Google Home app instead of the older Nest app. Some users on Reddit mention mixed opinions about this transition.

Is It Worth Buying?

A Nest thermostat is worth it if you:

Use central HVAC frequently
Want smart-home automation
Want remote control and scheduling
Care about energy savings

It may not be ideal if:

Your HVAC system is incompatible
You prefer local-only devices without cloud dependency
You want long-term offline functionality guarantees

For most users, the Learning Thermostat models are considered among the best smart thermostats available today.
