# Amazon Smart Thermostat – Demand Response & Energy Incentives Guide

## What Is a Demand Response Program?

Demand Response (DR) programs are utility-run initiatives that help balance electricity supply and demand during peak usage periods. When millions of people simultaneously run air conditioning during a heatwave, the electrical grid becomes strained — utilities must activate expensive, less eco-friendly "peaker" power plants to compensate.

Instead, utilities offer **bill credits and rewards** to customers who allow temporary, small thermostat adjustments during these peak events.

---

## How It Works with the Amazon Smart Thermostat

1. **Enroll** via the Alexa app → Devices → Thermostat → Device Settings → Energy Savings Incentives
2. During a "demand event," your utility (or their authorized service provider) temporarily adjusts your thermostat temperature
3. Adjustments are **gradual and small** — typically up to 4°F higher for cooling
4. You can **always manually override** the setting if uncomfortable
5. Events usually last up to **4 hours**, commonly between 11 AM and 11 PM on weekdays
6. Most programs run **10–25 events per summer season**, concentrated on the hottest days

> **Note:** You can only enroll in one energy savings incentives program per household. Multiple thermostats in the same home can each be enrolled in the same program.

## Financial Benefits

| Program Type | Typical Reward |
|---|---|
| Enrollment / sign-up rebate | $50–$100 one-time |
| Annual bill credits (demand response) | $20–$100+ per year |
| Utility rebate on device purchase | $50–$85 (varies by utility) |

### Example Utility Programs That Include Amazon Smart Thermostat:
- **Con Edison (NY):** $85 rebate on enrollment
- **Eversource:** $70+ earned within the first year
- **SCE (Southern California Edison):** Ongoing bill credits via Smart Energy Program
- **Various others:** Check your ZIP code at [energystar.gov/rebate-finder](https://www.energystar.gov/rebate-finder)

---

## How to Find Rebates in Your Area

1. Visit: **energystar.gov/rebate-finder**
2. Enter your ZIP code
3. Select "Smart Thermostats"
4. View all utility incentives available in your service area

Alternatively, go to **amazon.com → Energy Rebates** and search by ZIP code directly on the Amazon Smart Thermostat product page.

---

## Pre-Cooling Strategy

Many utilities use a "pre-cooling" approach before events start — your home is cooled slightly below your preferred temperature before the event window so you're comfortable throughout. Most participants report **little to no noticeable difference** in comfort.

---

## Unenrolling

You can unenroll anytime:
- Through the Alexa app
- Through your utility's portal or app
- By emailing your utility's program provider
